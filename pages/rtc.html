RTC | Real-time Clock

Sometimes a simple small PCB is more fun to design. This is a series of boards designed for the purpose of keeping time during power fluctuations or outages.

This first PCB is designed to sit on a Raspberry Pi. It has a slim profile and should fit inside most RPi cases. This board is designed for the DS1307 serial real-time clock. The DS1307 uses the I2C bus and consumes less than 500nA when the battery is powering the clock. The battery holder is large enough for a 20mm coin cell battery like the CR2032.

A second PCB was designed for the Arduino Due or Mega form factor but includes an inverter button debounce circuit for the Due's 3V3 logic level. This can be left unpopulated for the Mega. To minimize the board footprint a 3V3 regulator is included. It uses the 5V power rail for the 3V3 button indicator.
